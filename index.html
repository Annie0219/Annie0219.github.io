    Hi! I’m Yangyang (Annie) Li, a senior at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, double-majoring in Computer Science, Economics & Data Science (6-14) and Business Analytics (15-2). An award-winning researcher (Bain Cup Finalist, Regeneron STS Scholar, MSEF 2nd Place, Google Code Jam Top 1000), I’m fascinated by how learning systems can reason, adapt, and stay reliable under uncertainty.

    My current research centers on robust and adaptive intelligence. I developed DRO-InstructZero, a distributionally robust Bayesian-optimization framework for Large Language Models that explicitly maximizes worst-case reliability under domain shift — bridging robustness theory with prompt alignment. This work, under review at ICLR 2026, establishes a principled connection between distributionally robust optimization and instruction learning.

    In parallel, I co-authored Adaptive Virtual Model Control with LLM- and Lyapunov-based Reinforcement Learning, recently submitted to ICASSP 2026. The study combines large-language-model guidance with Lyapunov-constrained RL to achieve physically interpretable yet stability-guaranteed robotic control — demonstrating that semantic reasoning from LLMs can enhance safe adaptation in real-time machine control.

    Across both lines of work — from robust LLM optimization to adaptive robotics control — my goal is to design learning systems that remain trustworthy when the world changes. I hope to pursue a Ph.D. that advances reinforcement learning, Bayesian optimization, and AI robustness toward more reliable and interpretable intelligence.
